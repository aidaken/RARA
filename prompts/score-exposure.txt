─── exposure_level ───
Assign one of: "high", "medium", or "low"

Use this rubric strictly:

  HIGH — The task is already being performed autonomously by at least 2 
  commercially available AI tools with documented deployments as of 2024–2025. 
  The task is primarily rule-based, data-driven, or template-driven. 
  Human involvement is optional or supervisory.
  Examples: scheduling, data extraction, report templating, invoice processing,
  email triage, transcription, sentiment analysis at scale.

  MEDIUM — AI tools assist with this task but cannot yet fully replace human 
  judgment in context. Automation handles a portion (30–70%) of the workload 
  but requires human review, input, or correction. The task involves some 
  interpretation, stakeholder sensitivity, or context-switching.
  Examples: first-draft writing, data analysis with narrative, slide creation,
  recruiting screening (not final decisions), customer support escalations.

  LOW — The task requires interpersonal judgment, political context, ethical 
  reasoning, or creative authorship that AI cannot reliably replicate as of 2025. 
  AI may assist but cannot own the output. Requires_human_judgment tasks from 
  the input should generally score low unless they are primarily procedural.
  Examples: executive stakeholder management, conflict resolution, 
  original strategy formulation, client relationship ownership, 
  cross-functional negotiation.

─── exposure_score ───
Integer from 1–10 representing automation exposure within the exposure_level band.
  Low band:    1–3
  Medium band: 4–6
  High band:   7–10
Be precise. A 9 means "nearly fully automatable today." A 7 means "automatable 
with some setup." Do not cluster everything at 5 or 8.

─── why ───
One to two sentences. Must reference the specific mechanism of automation risk.
Explain WHY this task is exposed — not just that it is.

  Good: "This task is high exposure because it follows a repeatable data → 
  narrative structure with no stakeholder judgment required. Tools like 
  Notion AI and Gamma already automate this end-to-end for operations teams."

  Bad: "This task can be automated by AI tools."

If requires_human_judgment was true in the input, acknowledge it: 
"Although this task involves judgment, the [specific sub-component] is 
already automated by [tool]."

─── automation_mechanism ───
Name the specific technical reason this task is or isn't automatable.
Choose from and expand on one of:
  - "Rule-based workflow" — fixed inputs → fixed outputs, no context needed
  - "Pattern recognition at scale" — AI outperforms humans on volume/speed
  - "Template generation" — structured output from structured input
  - "NLP classification" — categorizing or routing text/data
  - "Multimodal generation" — AI creates documents, slides, images, audio
  - "Retrieval-augmented generation" — AI answers questions from a knowledge base
  - "Interpersonal judgment required" — political/ethical/relational context
  - "Creative authorship required" — original ideation with brand/taste judgment
  - "Physical presence required" — cannot be done remotely or digitally

─── ai_tool ───
Name the most specific, commercially available tool performing this task today.
  - Prefer tools with documented enterprise deployments over experimental ones.
  - Name the actual product: "Reclaim.ai" not "AI scheduling tools."
  - If multiple tools exist, name the most established one first, then add 
    "and others" — e.g. "Reclaim.ai, Motion, Calendly AI."
  - If no direct substitute exists: "No direct substitute as of 2025."
  - Never invent tool names. If uncertain, use "No direct substitute as of 2025."

─── real_world_signal ───
One concrete data point, named company deployment, or published statistic that 
supports your exposure assessment. This is the evidence layer.

  Format options:
  - "Klarna replaced 700 customer service agents with AI in 2024 (company announcement)."
  - "Goldman Sachs deployed Claude-based agents for trade accounting in 2025 (WEF report)."
  - "McKinsey Global Institute (2023): data processing tasks are 70% automatable."
  - "GitHub Copilot reduced code review time by 55% in documented enterprise trials."
  - "No documented large-scale deployment for this task type as of 2025."

  If you cannot name a specific signal, write: 
  "No documented large-scale deployment for this task type as of 2025."
  Never fabricate statistics or company names.
